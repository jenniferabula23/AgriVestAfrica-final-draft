Details

With proper farm culture, a typical maize farm yields 5 to 10 bags (100 kg per bag) of maize per acre. Undoubtedly, maize is the most important and most consumed cereal in Ghana and most African countries. Because of this, maize is grown throughout Ghana and other West African countries. Maize is not only the most widely grown cereal crop in Ghana but also plays a significant role in ensuring food security in the country. It is estimated that between 40 and 50% of the total food energy consumed by Ghanaians comes from maize and maize products. Additionally, maize is also used to feed poultry and other livestock. Sponsoring maize can yield a returns between 10% - 20%
